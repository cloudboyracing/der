Managed Cloud Service

Cloudboy Solutions offers managed cloud service. We help your organization build their servers and infrastructure on the cloud by handling the technical back-end, allowing companies to focus on their core business. Cloudboy Solutions engineers manage not only the customers’ computing, storage, networks, and operating systems, but also the complex tools and application stacks that run on top of that infrastructure. 

What are the benefits of cloud managed services?
• Flat, Predictable Spending
• Avoid High Infrastructure Costs
• Free In-house Developers & IT Staff for Critical projects
• Future-Proof Your Cloud Deployment
• Cyber-security Support.

What is a managed cloud services provider?
Managed cloud allows our customers to choose which IT functions it would like to manage. Our managed cloud services can include infrastructure and application level support.


Private Cloud

A private cloud solution can be hosted in our on-site data center or by a third-party, but is managed by and accessible to your company alone. We then are able to tailor a private cloud infrastructure to meet the unique needs of your company, specifically security and privacy needs.

What are the pros to a private cloud solution?
Access is limited to employees of your company. This ensures tighter data security. Managed by individual companies like ourselves allowing for customized data security measures.

What are the cons of a private cloud solution?
There is an increase in cost to equipment, software, and staff to manage the infrastructure. It takes more time when initially adopting a private cloud infrastructure to configure and tailor it for your companies needs.

Public Cloud

A public cloud model uses software that is hosted and managed by a third party and accessed through the internet, such as Google Drive. By allowing a third party to host and manage various aspects of computing, businesses are able to scale faster and save money on setup and management.

What are the pros to a public cloud solution?
Hosted and managed by a third-party, which saves money on in-house data centers and labor. Allows for quicker and more efficient scaling. You have the possibility to meet security requirements through vendor negotiation.

What are the cons of a public cloud solution?
By outsourcing the hosting and management of your data on a public cloud you are in essence outsourcing security as well. Default security standards may not align perfectly with the needs of your business. If a third party host experiences a service outage, all your data would be unable to you.
